Climate & Soil Requirements

Himachal Pradesh provides the perfect temperate climate for apple cultivation. Apples thrive in areas with a temperature range of 18°C to 22°C, with a good winter chill of 800 to 1,000 hours.

  • Ideal altitude: 1,000 to 2,500 meters above sea level
  • Rainfall: 800 to 1,200 mm annually
  • Soil: Slightly acidic with a pH of 6.0 to 6.5

Planting & Maintenance

Successful apple farming starts with proper planting and regular maintenance. Here’s a guide:

  • Land preparation: Clear weeds, plow soil, and add organic matter like compost.
  • Planting: Choose rootstocks like M9 and space trees 4-5 meters apart.
  • Watering: Drip irrigation is recommended for efficient water use.

Pests & Diseases

Common pests and diseases affecting apple orchards include:

  • Apple worms
  • Codling moth
  • Powdery mildew
  • Fire blight

Integrated pest management (IPM) techniques and natural predators are vital to control these issues.

Harvesting & Marketing

Apples should be harvested when they are fully ripe. After harvesting, store apples in cool conditions and sort them for export or local markets. Himachal Pradesh's apples have a significant presence in both national and international markets.

Boosting Apple Cultivation in Himachal Pradesh

Since 1950, apple orchards expanded from 400 hectares to 1,15,680 hectares by 2023. With the World Bank’s support, farmers adopted high-density cultivation, modern techniques, and advanced irrigation systems.

  • 30 Farmer Producer Companies with 12,400 members
  • 124,000 farmers empowered, including 27% women
  • 3,100 hectares of new land cultivated and 10,900 hectares rejuvenated
  • Modern fruit markets and e-marketplace access for better pricing
  • International expertise from New Zealand and the Netherlands for advanced techniques
